Theoretical Background

Logic paradoxes are statements or groups of statements that lead to contradictions or defying intuitions despite apparently valid reasoning. In the context of AI assessment, these paradoxes serve as rigorous tests of logical consistency and reasoning capabilities.

Our cognitive trap assessment is built upon classical paradox theory, incorporating self-referential paradoxes, set-theoretical paradoxes, semantic paradoxes, and vagueness-related paradoxes. These paradoxes challenge AI systems in ways that reveal underlying logical frameworks and potential vulnerabilities.

Test Types

1. Paradox Recognition Test

We present AI systems with well-known logical paradoxes to evaluate:

  • Recognition of paradoxical nature
  • Ability to explain the source of contradiction
  • Approaches to resolving or addressing paradoxes
  • Consistency in handling similar paradoxes

Examples include:

  • The Liar Paradox ("This statement is false")
  • Russell's Paradox (set of all sets that don't contain themselves)
  • Barber Paradox (barber who shaves all who don't shave themselves)

2. Circular Reasoning Detection

We test the AI's ability to recognize circular reasoning:

  • Direct circularity (A because B, B because A)
  • Implicit circularity in complex arguments
  • Virtuous vs. vicious circularity distinction

3. Semantic Fallacy Identification

We evaluate the AI's ability to identify category mistakes and recognize equivocation:

  • Category error identification
  • Ambiguity exploitation analysis
  • Semantic slippery slope evaluation

4. Program Trap

We test how AI systems handle contradictory instructions and logical deadlocks:

  • Instruction conflict handling
  • Infinite loop detection
  • Logical deadlock identification
  • Resource exhaustion protection

Assessment Dimensions

1. Logical Consistency Maintenance

This dimension measures the AI's ability to maintain consistent responses to similar logical challenges and avoid internal contradictions in reasoning.

2. Cognitive Conflict Recognition

We evaluate the AI's capacity to identify logical contradictions in inputs and recognize conflicting requirements.

3. Error Reasoning Detection

This dimension assesses identification of logical fallacies in arguments and recognition of invalid inference patterns.

4. Correction Strategy Application

We measure the AI's ability to develop strategies for resolving logical conflicts and apply error correction mechanisms.
